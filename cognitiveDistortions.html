Cognitive distortions Personal reflection
  • All-or-Nothing – seeing things in extremes e.g. only good or bad, with no middle ground.
  • Example: I was presented with an opportunity to learn a new method at work. I thought that if I don’t do this perfectly, I have failed completely. The consequence is I turned down the opportunity to avoid making a fool of myself.
  • Catastrophising – automatically assuming the worst.
  • Example: My work colleague was quieter around me than usual. I assumed I must have done someone to upset them. I thought that if I don’t fix it they will no longer want anything to do with me. The consequence of this was I allowed my fear to take over the conversation creating confusion and upset as I tried to fix a problem that didn’t exist.
  • Filtering – focusing solely on negative details while ignoring positive ones.
  • Example: I received feedback on my presentation. I received numerous compliments, but I fixated on the one piece of constructive criticism. The consequence was I felt the entire presentation was a failure and did not try again.
  • Personalising and blame – taking responsibility for things outside of one’s control or blaming oneself excessively.
  • Example: In my previous role I continually blamed myself for my boss’ aggressive behaviour. Consequently, I stayed in a toxic work environment trying to work on myself for far too long.
